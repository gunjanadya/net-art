Fake Student IG

Instagram, a widely used photo-sharing app, plays a significant role in shaping our social interactions by providing a platform for users to share moments of their lives through images. However, its impact raises questions about authenticity due to the ease with which fabricated content can be disseminated. To explore this, I conducted an intervention by creating a fake profile of a Rutgers student named "Brianna" using images sourced exclusively from Pinterest. From Friday, March 8th, to Sunday, March 10th, I followed numerous Rutgers students with the aim of assessing their interactions with the fake profile. Surprisingly, some private accounts, which require owner approval for follow requests, accepted the request, indicating a level of perceived authenticity and trust. This acceptance suggests that users may have genuinely believed the fake profile represented someone they knew or had encountered, underscoring the potential for deception and manipulation on social media platforms like Instagram. This analysis highlights the need for users to approach online interactions with caution and critical thinking. With more time, I would continue this intervention by interacting with some of these users to see if I could get them to reveal more personal information to my fake account.
