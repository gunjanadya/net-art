r/place

r/place was a collaborative art project hosted on Reddit in April 2017, and continued on annually after that. It consists of a large, blank canvas where users can place a single colored pixel every few minutes. The canvas is divided into a grid, and users can create, modify, or destroy pixel art in real-time. The project attracts millions of users who create intricate artworks, fight for control over certain areas, and collaborate to create larger designs. r/place demonstrates the power of online communities to create and organize complex projects in real-time. Can you spot the Rutgers "R" in last year's canvas?

Exhausting a Crowd

Exhausting a Crowd presents footage from seven major cities and allows users to add moving captions to this footage. These captions are added into the site as a whole so that they are viewable by all users. This adds dimentionality to these videos, as captions are often tracking particular peoople in the crowd and assigning them thoguhts, feelings, and words. In this way, the online crowd creates an interesting and realistic crowd in person in these clips. What do you think they're thinking in there?

Tokenize This

Tokenize This was created as a response to the popularity of NFTs. Each reload of the site creates a new peice of art, that cannot ever be viewable again. This demonstrates the absurdity of creating false scarcity over digital art. Furthermore, it demonstrates the fleeting nature of internet art. The lack of physical space taken up by this art means it can be infinite, but it also goes away the second we step away from the screen.
